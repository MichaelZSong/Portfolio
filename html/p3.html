Overview:

The Thunder game interface design was a ten-week team project in the course Interaction Design Methods. We were told to get in touch with an organization or company, familiarize them through meetings and researches, then analyze their design problem to deliver a solution for the problem at the end. This project examined our concepts of design practice and the related design methods. After reaching out to many organizations, we eventually decided on working with the IMU Studios team.

Research Phase:

After deciding on our partner organization, we needed to get to know them. Therefore, we arranged a meeting with IMU Studios' founder, Robert Strutin. Although we had to use Discord to conduct the meeting due to COVID-19 restrictions, it went really well because Robert was extremely polite to us and explained to us his game and how he got to where he is right now. This meeting was very effective and laid out a strong foundation for our research of his company.

Posters:

After the meeting, my partners and I started to organize the information we collected from the meeting. I was also in charge of the design of the posters. Once I went through their company website and the videos of their game development progress multiple times, I was able to establish a clear understanding of their game Thunder: Throne Wars and create two posters in Figure 1 to outline our project.

Figure 2. Sebastian Wu (2021). IMU Studios User Journey. [Poster].

User Journey Map:

To figure out the process that the player has to go through in the game, we set up another meeting with the IMU Studios team. Different from last time, this meeting is a participatory workshop with three activities. The activities helped align us with the team in terms of the menus, colours, and the style of the interface elements they have in mind. We settled on the idea of making the start menu interface for them because it will help push their game forward to the next stage of development. The user journey map in Figure 2 was then created to pitch the idea to our instructors.

Figure 3. Michael Song (2021). Thunder Test Interface. [Mockup].

The Game Interface:

With the approval from our course instructors, we started to move forward towards our final concept mockup design. We wanted to provide Thunder: Throne Wars with a more consistent design direction, such as creating a hierarchy between elements, a cohesive theme and a solid colour palette. We also wanted to create designs that can be polished and built on by future art directors. Furthermore, with tons of new information to take in, such as in-game mechanics, events, items, characters, we wanted to display information only when required or the player wants to view it. With those things in mind, I created a test interface in Figure 3. However, I quickly realized that it looks too blend since it doesn't have any unique elements. As a result, I restarted the progress with a more innovative approach.

Figure 4. Michael Song (2021). Thunder Game World Spawn Interface. [Mockup].
